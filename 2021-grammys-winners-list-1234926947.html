Grammys 2021: The Complete Winners List

July 2024 · 26 minute read

This year’s Grammy Awards are finally here.

Originally set for Jan. 31, tonight’s Trevor Noah-hosted ceremony will be broadcast on CBS, from the Los Angeles Convention Center, beginning at 5 p.m. PT/8 p.m. ET. The untelevised pre-telecast begins at 12 p.m. PT. The show’s lineup boasts an impressive 22 performers, including Harry Styles, Taylor Swift, Dua Lipa, BTS, Billie Eilish, Post Malone and Megan Thee Stallion. First-time Grammys executive producer Ben Winston tells Variety that the performances will be a mix of live and pre-recorded sets, and will take place from four stages, each set up in-the-round style.

Though the Grammys will not have its usual audience due to the pandemic, each performance stage will be surrounded by performers, nominees and guests, all of whom will be regularly tested for COVID-19. A fifth stage will be used by the show’s presenters, many of whom are venue personnel from the Apollo in New York, the Station Inn in Nashville, and the Troubadour and Hotel Café in L.A.

Those with four noms include Eilish, Megan, Phoebe Bridgers, Justin Bieber, DaBaby, jazz musician John Beasley and classical composer David Frost.
