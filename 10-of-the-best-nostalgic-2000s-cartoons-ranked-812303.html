10 of the Best Nostalgic 2000s Cartoons, Ranked

September 2024 · 4 minute read

The early 2000s brought us some of the best cartoons of all time. The best of them had intricate storylines and nuanced characters that appealed to viewers of all ages. However, some early 2000s cartoons have been forgotten and didn’t receive as much attention then but are worth revisiting. This article will take you on a trip down memory lane as you explore the early 2000s cartoon landscape, including the best and forgotten gems.

1. Avatar: The Last Airbender

Considered one of the best 2000s cartoons, Avatar: The Last Airbender is a beloved classic that continues to inspire and entertain fans of all ages. The show’s intricate storytelling, captivating world-building, and memorable characters make it stand out among the best cartoons of the 2000s.

Although not as well-known as some of its contemporaries, Avatar: The Last Airbender is a forgotten gem that deserves to be rediscovered and cherished by fans of 2000s cartoons. With its blend of action, humor, and heart, this show is a timeless masterpiece that continues to inspire new generations of fans.

2. The Fairly OddParents

The early 2000s were a golden age for cartoons, and The Fairly OddParents was one of the best cartoons to emerge from that era. Despite being one of the forgotten 2000s cartoons in recent years, it remains a beloved classic among fans of the best ’00s cartoons.

The show’s whimsical premise of fairy godparents and their antics with their young charge struck a chord with viewers of all ages. With its colorful animation and memorable characters, The Fairly OddParents is a must-watch for anyone looking to revisit the best cartoons of the early 2000s.

3. What’s New, Scooby-Doo?

What’s New? Scooby-Doo stood out among the best 2000s cartoons and is a true classic among the forgotten cartoons of that time. Its updated animation and modernized storytelling made it a fan favorite in the early 2000s era. What’s New? Scooby-Doo remains one of the best 2000s cartoons, offering a unique blend of humor, suspense, and nostalgia that has stood the test of time.

4. SpongeBob SquarePants

SpongeBob SquarePants is undoubtedly one of the best 2000s cartoons that captured young and old viewers’ hearts. The show’s zany characters, hilarious plots, and vibrant animation style made it stand out among one of the best 2000s cartoons. For those who grew up during this era, SpongeBob SquarePants is a timeless classic that has earned its place in the early 2000s cartoon history.

5. Futurama

Futurama, the animated series set in the thirty-first century, was one of the most popular shows of the early 2000s. While it had its fair share of loyal fans, it often gets forgotten amidst the other cartoons of this time. However, with its clever writing, hilarious jokes, and lovable characters, it remains one of the best 2000s cartoons of all time.

Even though it may have been forgotten amidst 2000s cartoons, it has remained a fan favorite thanks to its iconic characters, catchy theme song, and action-packed storylines. That all being said, among the most thrilling 2000s cartoons, Kim Possible should be on our watchlist.

7. Phineas and Ferb

Despite being one of the best 2000s cartoons known for its witty humor and catchy songs, Phineas and Ferb often gets overlooked as one of the most beloved early 2000s cartoons. While it is still being produced, unlike some other 2000s cartoons, it still holds a special place in the hearts of many fans as one of the best cartoons of its time.

8. Teen Titans

The early 2000s brought about some of the best cartoons of all time, and Teen Titans were no exception. It’s hard not to feel nostalgic when thinking about the adventures of Robin, Starfire, Raven, Cyborg, and Beast Boy. Even though it may be a forgotten 2000s cartoon to some, it will always hold a special place in the hearts of those who grew up with it as one of the best 2000s cartoons of their childhood.

10. Courage the Cowardly Dog

Courage the Cowardly Dog may have been one of the forgotten 2000s cartoons, but it remains one of the best cartoons from that era. Its unique blend of horror and humor captivated audiences and left a lasting impression on those who watched it. Courage and his adventures will always hold a special place in the hearts of fans of early 2000s cartoons.
